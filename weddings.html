FAQ

Wedding questions,
answered honestly.

If something isn't covered here, just reach out. We're easy to get in touch with.

Do you provide MCs for weddings? +

Yes. Our DJs handle full MC duties, including grand entrance, first dance, toasts, all of it, or play a supporting role if you have a coordinator running the flow. Either way, we get the run of show ahead of time so we're never winging announcements or killing the energy with an awkward pause.

Can you do both the ceremony and reception? +

That's actually how most of our weddings work. We set up ceremony mics and audio first, cover cocktail hour, then flip into full reception mode: DJ, lighting, effects, the whole thing. One crew the entire night means no handoff, no "the other vendor said..." conversations. Everything just flows.

Are cold sparks and fog safe indoors? +

Cold sparks don't produce real heat. It's a visual effect, not actual fire. You can stand right next to them. Dancing on cloud uses a low-lying water-based fog that stays at knee height and clears out fast. It won't trigger smoke alarms, won't bother guests with asthma, and looks incredible on camera. We've used both in all kinds of South Florida venues: ballrooms, rooftops, outdoor tents. Zero issues.

How far in advance should I book for my wedding? +

Wedding season in South Florida runs October through April. For peak season dates, 4–6 months out is the sweet spot. Summer weddings have more flexibility. Either way, once you have your date, reach out. Popular weekends fill quickly.

What if it rains during an outdoor ceremony? +

We plan for it. All our outdoor equipment is weather-rated, and we always discuss contingency plans in advance: a tent plan, an indoor backup space, or just knowing how quickly we can move gear under cover, an indoor backup space, or just knowing how quickly we can move gear under cover. South Florida weather is unpredictable, but we're not.
