Custom gobo projection — your name, monogram, logo, or pattern on the walls, the floor, the ceiling. Guests don't always know what a gobo is. They just know the room looks different from any other event they've been to. And every photo from the night looks like it was designed.

A gobo is a custom-cut metal template that fits into a stage lighting fixture and projects a sharp image wherever the light is aimed. It's how you get your initials on the dance floor or your company logo on the back wall without printing a banner.

We can project names, monograms, logos, geometric patterns, floral designs, or anything else that can be rendered as a high-contrast image. Custom gobos are fabricated for your event. Standard gobos from our library are also available.

Placement makes the difference. A well-positioned gobo projection on a textured wall creates depth and dimension that photographs differently from every angle. We scout your venue and recommend positions that maximize visual impact.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does gobo projection cost in Miami?

Gobo and monogram projection in Miami typically ranges from $350 to $900 depending on gobo type, projection distance, and whether a custom design is needed. Baseline Studio includes setup and on-site positioning in every package — contact us for a quote.

What is a gobo and how does it work?

A gobo is a metal or glass template that fits inside a specialized light fixture. When projected, it casts a sharp image — a name, logo, monogram, or pattern — onto walls, floors, or ceilings. The result is a custom branded element that appears as part of your venue's environment, not just a prop.

Can you project our wedding monogram or company logo?

Yes. We produce custom metal gobos from any design you provide — a couples' monogram, company logo, hashtag, or decorative pattern. We need your artwork 2–3 weeks before the event so the gobo can be fabricated. We can also use stock patterns if you don't have a custom design.

What surfaces can the gobo be projected onto?

Gobos project best onto flat, light-colored surfaces — white or off-white walls, dance floors, ceilings, and draping. Textured or dark surfaces reduce sharpness. We scout the venue before the event to find the ideal projection surface and placement for maximum visual impact.

Can gobo projection be used alongside uplighting?

Yes — gobo projection and uplighting are frequently used together. Uplighting transforms the room's color and mood, while a gobo projection adds a personalized focal point. Baseline Studio manages both as part of a single lighting package, so everything is coordinated and on-brand.
