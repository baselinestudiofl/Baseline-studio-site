## General

**Where does Baseline Studio work?**

**Is Baseline Studio insured?**

For weddings during peak season (October through April in South Florida), 4–6 months out is the sweet spot. Some popular weekends in November and December book out earlier. Yes, and it makes everything smoother. We share the run of show, align load-in times with the venue, and stay in communication leading up to the event. Planners don't need to manage us on the day — introduce us, and we take it from there.

## Equipment & Setup

**What equipment does Baseline Studio use?**
DJ: Pioneer CDJ-3000 media players and DJM-A9 mixer. Sound: QSC and JBL line arrays with subwoofers. Lighting: LED uplights, moving head stage fixtures. LED walls: P3.9 indoor panels. We use touring-grade gear across the board — not consumer equipment.

**What's the backup equipment policy?**
We bring backup gear for every critical system — amplifiers, DJ controllers, mixers, and microphone receivers. Equipment issues have never stopped one of our shows. That's not luck — that's preparation.

**Can Baseline Studio handle outdoor events?**
Yes. All equipment is rated for outdoor South Florida conditions — humidity, heat, and salt air. We build weather contingency plans into every outdoor event. If rain is a possibility, we have a plan before we show up.

**How long does setup take?**
DJ-only: 1–2 hours. Full production (sound + LED walls + lighting + effects): 4–6+ hours. Setup time is always coordinated with the venue so guests never see the process.

## Special Effects

**Are cold spark fountains safe indoors?**
Yes. Cold spark effects use a titanium-based compound rather than traditional pyrotechnics. They produce no fire, no heat hazard, no smoke, and minimal residue. They are safe for use inside most South Florida venues. We confirm with your venue before the event.

**What's the difference between cold spark fountains and CO2 jets?**
Cold sparks produce a gold sparkler effect — fountain-shaped, about 4–6 feet high. CO2 jets produce a vertical burst of cold carbon dioxide gas, 10–15 feet high. Different visual, different energy. Cold sparks are typically used for first dances and toasts. CO2 jets are used for dancefloor energy moments.

**Is dancing on cloud the same as a fog machine?**
No. A fog machine produces atmospheric haze that rises and spreads throughout a room. Dancing on cloud uses chilled water vapor that stays at knee level and dissipates quickly. There's no ceiling fog, no smoke smell, and no fire alarm risk when used correctly.
